About & How To Use

What is Squawkar?

The home for opinions, debate, views and comment.

If you have something to say but nowhere to say it then Squawkar is for you.

It’s the world’s first online, wholly opinion-based website, the natural home for people who want to get something off their chests and share with the World.

Squawkar is an online community for the sardonic and witty, where malcontents and miseries can go to moan and mither, where the opinionated and the frustrated have a platform to rant, rail and vent their spleen.

It’s the place to go for witticisms, insightful verbal stings and satirical comment. And when users have had their fill of those, they can seek out debate, discussion and dialogue. Because Squawkar provides users with a voice in a world where, increasingly, people feel invisible, overlooked and ignored.

“I am as mad as hell and I am not going to take this anymore” (Peter Finch- Network 1974)

To Squawk means to bitch, bleat, carp, caterwaul, crab, croak, fuss, gripe, grizzle, grouch, growl, grumble, grump, holler, kick, kvetch, moan, murmur, mutter, nag, protest, scream, complain, squeal, wail, whimper, whine, whinge, yammer, yawp, yowl and thunder.

Squawk by:

1) Text

  • Title: Add a title and try to make it as attention-grabbing as possible to capture the reader’s attention and also to contain the key words for those searching. You can also add hashtags, which as per Twitter, enable more focussed searches.

  • Message: Here you can type what you want to type but please do not squawk or comment anything that may be illegal, harmful, obscene, threatening, discriminatory, abusive, harassing or libellous. Squawkar’s intention is to stay in existence, and breaking these laws will only result in the site crashing down. To protect itself, Squawkar has to reserve the right to remove, without notice and without giving any reason, any squawks or comments as it sees fit in contravention of these guidelines.

2) Graffiti

  • This is your chance to share a quick thought, rant or rave and your typing is transformed into graffiti spray.

3) Image

  • Here you can upload a picture of whatever amazes, amuses or makes you angry..


How To Use Squawkar

Squawkar has been designed to be as user-friendly and intuitive as possible. Social media users will find many familiar features.

  • Home Page

  • the latest squawks, search options, account access and of course the “squawk” button for self expression.

  • Creating an account

  • This is very straightforward and users can use their Facebook or Twitter details to log in. Otherwise, details can be entered following onscreen prompts and access is instant.

  • Users can change their password, add an avatar if they choose, give themselves an interesting username all by clicking “settings” on the username dropdown menu.

Features

  • Latest

  • Popular

  • Most Commented

  • Search by category and hashtags

  • Variety of Topic Subject Matters

  • Following - follow a contributor: click on the name which will open a page of all their squawks, press the “Follow” key and each time users log-in there will be a “Following” option to access.

  • Links to Facebook, Twitter, Email etc.

  • The usual 'like' and 'dislike' buttons – the ability to 'like' or positively endorse someone else's comment

  • Email notification - notifications to users if squawk comments are ‘liked’ and/or added to favourites
